The University of Santo Tomas (UST) is a private, Catholic research university in Manila, founded in 1611, making it the oldest university in Asia. ### **B. Campus & Daily Life: The Golden Cage and Waterworld**

The physical campus of UST is a dual experience: it is both a beautiful sanctuary and a legendary flood zone. The grounds are universally adored by students. Walking under the centuries-old trees or seeing the Main Building lit up at night provides a "dopamine hit" that helps mitigate the stress of the heavy workload.

However, "flood culture" is a genuine part of the Thomasian identity. Navigating waist-deep water on EspaÃ±a Boulevard or being stranded inside the campus during a typhoon is a "badge of honor". While drainage has improved, you will still find yourself playing the "suspension game," waiting for late-night announcements from an administration often criticized for disregarding commuter safety.

### **C. Social & Cultural Fit: The Social Chameleon**

UST students pride themselves on being "social chameleons". Unlike the perceived elitism of Ateneo or La Salle, UST is a melting pot. You will find students from wealthy families mixing effortlessly with scholars and those from the middle class.

The social vibe is generally more grounded and "down-to-earth". The mandatory uniform, while often hated for its restriction, acts as a "great equalizer" that hides class distinctions.

### **D. Identity & Values: Tradition vs. Repression**

Being a Thomasian means navigating a deeply Catholic environment. You will take mandatory Theology classes and hear the Angelus bell daily. If you are not Catholic, you will generally be respected by peers, but you must still follow institutional rituals like retreats and mass.

The biggest cultural friction at UST is between a modernizing student body and a conservative administration. The **Office for Student Affairs (OSA)** is frequently villainized for repressive policies. This came to a head in 2024 with the **TomasinoWeb 7-Eleven incident**, where a student media organization was forced to take down a photo because students in uniform supposedly "resembled 7-Eleven employees". This incident highlighted an administration often perceived as more obsessed with its pristine image than with student freedom of expression.

### **E. Organizations & Career: Professionalism and "Palakasan"**

Joining a top-tier student organization like the *Varsitarian* or the Central Student Council is described as a "bloodbath". The application processes are rigorous, often mimicking corporate interviews. However, there are frequent complaints about "Palakasan" (nepotism), where leadership roles allegedly go to those with the right connections.

Despite the internal politics, being a UST graduate carries significant weight in the professional world. Employers often view Thomasians as "resilient" and "trainable" because the university environment forces you to navigate heavy workloads and bureaucratic rigidity.
